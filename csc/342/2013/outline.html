CSCI 2342 Topic Outline by Week
(Always tentative, so check frequently for updates.)

Assigned Reading
We will begin this course by discussing a few more STL features before continuing our discussion of sorting algorithms (recursive sorting algorithms in particular). We will also, from time to time, revisit and extend some of the topics from the previous course in various contexts (stacks and queues, in particular). Some additional specific C++ topics will be discussed, including some from Chapter 8 of the text, input and output with binary files, and the remaining STL container classes. Then we will carry on with Chapters 10-14 of the text, looking at trees in general, expressions trees, binary search trees, heaps and priority queues, tables and hashing, and (depending on available time) graphs, C++ inheritance and polymorphism with virtual functions and multiple inheritance.

For those who need a C++ refresher, see Appendix A and Appendix C of the text.

Progress Assessment Quizzes
Students may expect short quizzes on material previously covered in the course at any time. These quizzes are intended to bring to the attention of students some of the things they should know, and to let the instructor know if, in fact, students are acquainted with the material. They may also serve as an "early warning system" to indicate the kinds of questions and their format that students will see on the mid-term test and final exam. These quizzes will not be awarded any grade points, but may be taken into account in a student's favor, at the end of the term, to help compensate for a poor performance on the evaluation items that do count, if this is warranted by the student's performance on those quizzes.
