Overview

This week you will design and write (another?) program to encrypt and decrypt textfiles. Such programs are nice exercises because they always involve making good choices for the structures and algorithms you need to use, and they require you (or should require you) to think through your approach before starting in a way that some other exercises do not.

Also, because you now have quite a number of STL facilities at your disposal, you have quite a variety of things to choose from when solving any problem with a C++ program, including this one. So, feel free to choose and use whatever works, from the STL and (of course) from the utilities.h/.obj package. In fact, think of this as another opportunity to get your "STL chops" up to speed.

Steps to Perform

  1. Copy the demo executable and the accompanying text items file that it needs in order to run.
  2. Study the program description (especially the encryption scheme description) you get by choosing option 2 from the menu.
  3. Make up a few short (or long, if you like) textfiles and try encrypting and decrypting them. Enter different phrases for doing so from they keyboard and from a file. Do this until you understand how the program works.
  4. Write pseudocode for a solution to this problem, and revise it until you are convinced that a program that implements your pseudocode will be the one you want.
  5. Translate your pseudocode into C++ and continue testing your program until you are convinced it works correctly.
  6. Make sure your source code is identified, formatted, and documented properly, according to our current rules and guidelines.
  7. Finally, submit the required files over the web.

Additional Notes, Requirements, Specifications and/or Hints (if any)

  1. The phrase used for encrypting/decrypting must be just a single line of text.
  2. To compare two files (as in option 6 on the menu), your program need only use the system fc command (for "file compare"). Your program should build the appropriate C-string containing this command and the names of the two files to be compared, and then pass it as the parameter in a call to the system() function.
  3. Don't forget to include in your testing files that are encrypted by your program and decrypted by the sample executable, and vice versa.
